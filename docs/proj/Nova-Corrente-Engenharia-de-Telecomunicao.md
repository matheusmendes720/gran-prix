# Datasets for Logistics and Demand Forecasting in Telecom Tower Maintenance

Based on your interest in Nova Corrente's telecommunications tower maintenance operations, here's a comprehensive overview of available datasets for logistics and demand forecasting in the same niche:

## Telecom-Specific Datasets

### Network Operations \& Infrastructure Datasets

**Telecom Network Dataset (Kaggle)**[^2_1]
This dataset focuses on tower-level operations with key features including tower identification, users connected per tower, download speed metrics, and network performance indicators. It's ideal for analyzing tower capacity utilization and service quality patterns.

**Cell Towers Worldwide Location Data**[^2_2]
An extensive geographic dataset containing coordinates and network information for cell tower locations globally, organized by continent. This dataset is valuable for spatial analysis, coverage optimization, and logistics route planning for maintenance operations.

**5G3E Dataset (5G End-to-End Emulation)**[^2_3]
Contains time-series system metrics from a virtualized 5G infrastructure over 14 days, created using real commercial cellular network traffic. Features include radio front-end metrics, physical server data, operating system metrics, and network function performance. The dataset includes thousands of features grouped by resource type and node type, making it excellent for predictive maintenance and demand forecasting.[^2_3]

**OpenCellid Dataset (40+ Million Records)**[^2_4]
A massive dataset with over 40 million rows containing cell tower features, latitude/longitude locations, radio technology types, signal range, and coverage data. This uses Dask for parallel processing and Datashader for visualization due to its scale.[^2_4]

### Predictive Maintenance \& Equipment Failure Datasets

**Equipment Failure Prediction Dataset (Kaggle)**[^2_5]
Contains 10,000 data points with 14 features including machine failure indicators, operational parameters, and failure types (hardware failures like CPU, memory, card, port issues, and software failures). Applicable to telecom equipment monitoring.[^2_6][^2_5]

**IoT-Integrated Predictive Maintenance Dataset**[^2_7]
Designed for predictive maintenance and equipment health monitoring, capturing realistic operational behavior with both normal and fault conditions. Integrates IoT sensor data streams relevant to tower equipment monitoring.

**Network Fault Prediction Dataset (Telstra)**[^2_8]
Built on Telstra's Network Disruption data to predict fault severity in telecommunications network disruptions, using multiple features to classify failure types and severity levels.[^2_8]

**5G Network Failure Prediction Dataset**[^2_9]
A multivariant time-series dataset from a real 5G core network testbed including metrics from cAdvisor, eBPF fine-grained metrics, and 5G logs. Contains 600 training test cycles and 300 test cycles with normal and abnormal states, designed specifically for predicting network failures with detection times as low as 10 seconds.[^2_9]

## Logistics \& Supply Chain Datasets

### Field Service \& Route Optimization

**Logistics Company Dataset for SQL (Kaggle)**[^2_10]
Comprehensive logistics records including shipment details, customer information, employee details, payment records, and delivery status. Contains data about goods content, international and domestic services, and cost management across strategic, tactical, and operational levels.[^2_10]

**Smart Logistics Supply Chain Dataset**[^2_11]
Real-time data capturing various aspects of supply chain management over 2024, including delivery performance, routing efficiency, warehouse operations, and resource allocation patterns.[^2_11]

**Transportation and Logistics Tracking Dataset**[^2_12]
Multiple datasets related to transportation operations including vehicle tracking, delivery times, route optimization data, and logistics performance metrics.[^2_12]

**Logistics Shipment On-Time Delivery Dataset**[^2_13]
Contains 1,000 synthetic shipment records with attributes including origin/destination cities, shipment type (Land/Air/Sea), weight, distance, customer segment, and on-time delivery indicator. Useful for building classification models to predict delivery performance.[^2_13]

### Supply Chain Optimization Data

**Supply Chain Shipment Pricing Data (Kaggle)**[^2_14]
Dataset focused on freight costs, shipping routes, delivery times, and pricing structures. Includes variables for optimizing logistics costs and delivery efficiency.[^2_15][^2_14]

**DataCo Supply Chain Dataset**[^2_16]
Comprehensive supply chain data including shipment patterns, carrier performance, freight movement, and logistics KPIs. Features quote IDs, origin/destination details, shipment dates, carrier names, equipment types, freight costs, weight, distance, fuel surcharges, and transit times.[^2_15]

## Demand Forecasting Datasets

### Telecommunications Demand \& Traffic Forecasting

**Milan Telecom \& Weather Dataset (Zenodo)**[^2_17]
Aggregated telecommunications and weather data from Milan covering November 2013 onwards. Used for resource demand prediction for network slices in 5G using machine learning. Combines traffic patterns with environmental factors.[^2_18][^2_17]

**Brazil Data Traffic Demand Forecast Dataset**[^2_19]
Contains broadband users with connections exceeding 34 Mbps, 4G/5G service prevalence, per capita GDP evolution, demographic changes, and online services consumption. Projects data consumption from 297.01 to 400.74 exabytes per year by 2033.[^2_19]

**5G Commercial Deployment Dataset**[^2_20][^2_21]
Real-world 5G deployment data from commercial networks with actual users, designed for forecasting techniques to understand trends and manage network resources. Includes traffic patterns, user behavior, and network performance metrics.[^2_22]

## Tower Maintenance Specific Data Sources

### Inspection \& Condition Monitoring

**Telecom Tower Defect Detection Dataset**[^2_23]
Focused on visual inspection data for tower components, particularly missing bolts and structural defects. Uses deep learning for automated defect detection from drone footage and manual inspections.[^2_23]

**Cell Tower Geolocation \& Maintenance Records**[^2_24][^2_25]
Open-source datasets combining Mozilla Location Service offline database, OpenCellId database, and crowdsourced measurements for tower localization and maintenance tracking.[^2_25][^2_24]

**Drone-Based Tower Inspection Data**[^2_26]
GIS-integrated drone technology data for cell tower maintenance, including structural imagery, geospatial coordinates, and inspection records.[^2_27][^2_26]

## Operations \& Maintenance (O\&M) Framework Data

**TelOps AI-Driven O\&M Dataset**[^2_28][^2_29]
First comprehensive AI-driven operations and maintenance framework for telecommunications networks, addressing topological dependence, heterogeneous software, and failure data. Includes:

- Risk discovery and assessment metrics
- Preventive maintenance indicators
- Failure diagnosis logs (most common O\&M task representing 50%+ of industrial telecom O\&M)[^2_28]
- Alarm information and root cause analysis data
- Mobile Access Network failure datasets


## Workforce Management Datasets

**Telecommunications Workforce Statistics (US Bureau of Labor Statistics)**[^2_30]
Employment data including 597,600 telecommunications employees (Aug 2025), occupational distribution, union membership (9.0% in 2024), and 35,212 establishments. Includes data on telecommunications equipment installers, line installers, and first-line supervisors.[^2_30]

**Field Service Optimization Data**
While specific public datasets are limited, workforce management data typically includes:[^2_31][^2_32]

- Technician skill levels and certifications
- Work order completion times
- Travel times between sites
- Part-time and gig worker availability
- Work-from-home productivity metrics


## Key Recommendations for Nova Corrente's Niche

Given Nova Corrente's focus on tower maintenance and O\&M operations, the most relevant datasets are:

1. **For Predictive Maintenance**: The 5G3E dataset, Equipment Failure Prediction dataset, and TelOps framework data provide comprehensive failure prediction capabilities[^2_5][^2_28][^2_3]
2. **For Logistics Optimization**: Combine cell tower location data with logistics tracking datasets to optimize truck rolls and maintenance crew routing[^2_2][^2_4][^2_12][^2_13]
3. **For Demand Forecasting**: The Milan telecom dataset, 5G commercial deployment data, and Brazil traffic forecast data enable capacity planning and maintenance scheduling[^2_17][^2_20][^2_19]
4. **For Tower-Specific Operations**: Tower defect detection data, drone inspection datasets, and OpenCellid records support condition-based maintenance strategies[^2_26][^2_4][^2_23]

Most datasets are available in CSV, JSON, or Parquet formats and can be accessed through Kaggle, GitHub, Zenodo, or institutional repositories. Many include Python notebooks and analysis scripts for immediate implementation in predictive models and optimization algorithms.

## Clarification and Refinement of Nova Corrente's Demand Forecasting Requirements

Your project's foundation is solid‚Äîpredicting inventory shortages before stockouts occur is a high-impact business problem. However, as your consultation notes indicate, three critical conceptual adjustments are necessary to transform this from theory into a working system. Let me clarify each one and map it to appropriate datasets and methodologies.

### Critical Conceptual Adjustments

#### 1. **AI Predicts DEMAND, Not Inventory Levels**

This is the fundamental distinction that many projects misunderstand. Your AI model should focus exclusively on forecasting **daily demand** (consumption patterns), not inventory states.

**What this means for Nova Corrente:**

- The model learns: "How many splice connectors are typically consumed per day across all maintenance sites?"
- The model does NOT learn: "What will the warehouse inventory level be tomorrow?"
- Your software layer then uses demand predictions to calculate inventory projections

**Why it matters:**

- Demand is a predictable business signal (seasonal patterns, maintenance cycles, holiday effects)
- Inventory is a derived metric that depends on demand + replenishment actions + stockouts
- Separating concerns makes your model more robust and reusable


#### 2. **Alert at Reorder Point (PP), Not at Minimum Stock Level** ‚ö†Ô∏è **MOST CRITICAL**

This is where many inventory systems fail. Triggering alerts only when you reach minimum stock is too late because suppliers need time to deliver.

**The Reorder Point Formula:**

\$ PP = (D_{daily} \times LT) + SS \$

Where:

- $D_{daily}$ = Average daily demand (from your AI forecast)
- $LT$ = Lead time in days (supplier delivery time)
- $SS$ = Safety stock (buffer for demand variability)

**Example for Tower Equipment:**

- Daily demand: 5 units
- Lead time: 14 days (supplier delivery)
- Safety stock: 20 units (2-week buffer)
- **Reorder Point = (5 √ó 14) + 20 = 90 units**

The procurement team should receive an alert when inventory drops to 90 units, giving 14 days for delivery before hitting the 20-unit safety floor.

**For Nova Corrente's O\&M Operations:**
Different maintenance sites and equipment types will have different lead times:[^3_1]

- Local warehouse parts: 1-2 days lead time
- Regional supplier: 7-10 days
- International manufacturers: 21-45 days

This means your system needs a **lead time matrix** by supplier and component type.

#### 3. **Forecast Granularity Must Be Daily (or Weekly at Minimum)**

Your business requirements mention "monthly control" but "weekly reports" and "7-day alerts." This is a mismatch.

**Why daily forecasting is necessary:**


| Requirement | Granularity Needed |
| :-- | :-- |
| Alert: "7 days until stockout" | Daily |
| Report: Weekly inventory status | Daily (aggregated weekly) |
| Reorder point trigger | Daily (constant comparison) |
| 30-day projection table | Daily (extrapolated 30 days forward) |

**For Your System:**

- Input: Daily consumption records per item
- Model output: 30-day daily demand forecast
- System logic: Daily recalculation of reorder points and alerts
- Reports: Weekly summaries and projections
